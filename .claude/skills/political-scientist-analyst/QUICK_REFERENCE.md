# Political Scientist Analyst - Quick Reference

## TL;DR

Analyzes events through political science lens using IR theory, comparative politics, institutional analysis, and power dynamics.

## When to Use

- International crises, conflicts, alliances
- Elections, regime transitions, democratization
- Policy decisions, institutional reforms
- Power struggles, governance issues

## Core Frameworks

1. **Realism** - Power, security, anarchy, balance of power
2. **Liberalism** - Institutions, cooperation, democratic peace
3. **Constructivism** - Norms, ideas, identities
4. **Comparative Politics** - Institutions, democracy vs. authoritarianism

## Levels of Analysis

- Individual (leaders)
- Domestic (regime, institutions, public opinion)
- Interstate (alliances, diplomacy)
- Systemic (power distribution, norms)

## Quick Analysis

1. **Define Event** - What happened? Who's involved?
2. **Power Analysis** - Who has power? What kind?
3. **Institutions** - What rules/norms apply?
4. **Actor Interests** - What do they want?
5. **Theory** - Realism, liberalism, or constructivism?
6. **Comparative** - Similar cases?
7. **Mechanisms** - How did it unfold?
8. **Implications** - Security, governance, policy?

## Key Thinkers

- Morgenthau (realism)
- Waltz (structural realism)
- Keohane (liberalism/institutions)
- Putnam (social capital)
